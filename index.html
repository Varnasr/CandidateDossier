About this tool

This project standardises how candidate dossiers are created across districts. You upload a CSV with candidate details, the tool generates LaTeX blocks—uniformly formatted content sections— and masks all phone numbers automatically. This ensures consistent structure, clean typography, and full privacy compliance under the Indian IT Act.

What the template should look like

The LaTeX template produces two parts:

  • Candidate Data Blocks — auto-generated from your CSV (names, caste, education, rationale, etc.)
  • Layout Block — the base file main.tex controlling margins, fonts, and section order.
Each block follows the same structure to keep dossiers comparable.

Why phone number masking is mandatory

Personal data in candidate lists is sensitive. Masking phone numbers protects privacy and complies with the Information Technology Act (2000) and related data protection rules. The masking process happens automatically during generation—you cannot disable it.

Process overview

  1. Upload your candidate CSV (see sample below).
  2. The system generates masked candidate_blocks.tex.
  3. Click “Download candidate data block” and “Download layout block.”
  4. In Overleaf: upload both, ensure \input{candidate_blocks.tex} appears in main.tex.
  5. Compile in Overleaf → get your formatted PDF.
  6. (Advanced users) Use Netlify serverless compile for one-click “Upload CSV → Get PDF”.
